Spaghetti Carbonara Recipe

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish known for its silky, creamy sauce made without creamâ€”just eggs, cheese, and pasta water. Crispy guanciale (or pancetta) adds salty richness, while black pepper brings a gentle heat. It's quick, elegant, and incredibly satisfying.

The magic lies in the emulsion of starchy pasta water with egg yolks and cheese, creating a velvety coating that clings perfectly to the spaghetti. Ready in under 30 minutes, it's perfect for weeknight dinners or a late-night craving. Serve with extra cheese and pepper for pure indulgence!

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 200g (7 oz) guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 100g (1 cup) Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated (plus extra for serving)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente according to package instructions.
  2. Meanwhile, in a large skillet over medium heat, cook the guanciale or pancetta until crispy and golden, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, grated Pecorino, and plenty of freshly ground black pepper until smooth.
  4. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the spaghetti.
  5. Add the hot spaghetti to the skillet with the guanciale over low heat. Toss to combine.
  6. Remove the skillet from heat. Quickly pour in the egg mixture and toss vigorously, adding reserved pasta water a splash at a time until a creamy sauce forms (it should coat the pasta without scrambling the eggs).
  7. Season with more pepper and salt if needed. Serve immediately with extra cheese.
